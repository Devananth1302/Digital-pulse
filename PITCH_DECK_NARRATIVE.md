• **Weighted Influence Scoring** — Patent-pending algorithm combining 4 factors (40% engagement intensity + 30% velocity + 15% recency + 15% semantic relevance) for predictive accuracy

• **24-Hour Virality Forecasts** — Predicts engagement trajectory with 70%+ accuracy, enabling proactive campaign planning

### 30-Second Script Snippet

*[Shift tone to confident, technical founder]*

---

"**Most platforms treat virality as a black box.**  

We approach it differently. We built **layered intelligence**.

If you're challenged on any of these points during your pitch, here are rapid-fire responses:

### "Why not just use Google Trends?"
> "Google Trends has 48-hour lag and zero predictive capability. We deliver real-time insights with 24-hour forecasts. Also, it's platform-agnostic—we aggregate Reddit, news, and custom sources, not just search volume."

### "The market is crowded with analytics tools."
> "Correct. But most are fragmented (platform-specific), delayed (batch, not real-time), or prohibitively expensive ($5K+/month). We're building for the untapped segment: creators and agencies who need real-time, affordable, hierarchical intelligence."

### "Aren't you dependent on these data sources' APIs?"
> "Smart question. Yes, but we mitigate it three ways: First, we support multiple sources—if one API breaks, others continue. Second, our Kafka architecture decouples sources from analytics. Third, we're building a creator SDK so users can submit their own data. Long-term, we own the distribution."

### "How do you forecast virality with 70% accuracy?"
> "We combine three signals: historical momentum patterns for the cluster, weighted influence scoring for the individual post, and time-decay functions because viral content has natural lifecycle. We validate against 6 months of historical data. Conservative, not magic."

### "What's your go-to-market strategy?"
> "Creator community first (Product Hunt, Reddit, TikTok creator forums). They're our true believers. Next, agency partnerships (integrate into agency tech stacks). Finally, enterprise sales (Fortune 500 research teams). We're strategic about sequencing."

## 📋 SLIDE DECK DIRECTOR'S NOTES

### Delivery Tips
1. **Slide 1 (Problem)**: Energy up. Act like you feel the pain. Make it real.
2. **Slide 2 (Innovation)**: Shift to "teacher" mode. You're explaining something cool, not selling.
3. **Slide 3 (Tech)**: Show live demo if possible. Even 10 seconds of the dashboard is worth 1000 words.
4. **Slide 4 (Market)**: Numbers matter. Speak with confidence. Let the data do the talking.
5. **Slide 5 (Model)**: Closer tone. This is where you establish you've thought through the business side.

### Pacing Cues
- **Total video time**: 3 minutes = 180 seconds
- **Slide 1**: Talk for exactly 60 seconds (not rushed, not slow)
- **Slide 2**: 45 seconds (medium energy)
- **Slide 3**: 30 seconds (technical but accessible)
- **Slide 4**: 30 seconds (data-driven confidence)
- **Slide 5**: 15 seconds (crisp, forward-looking)

### Visual Production Checklist
- [ ] Slide 1: Animated chaos (scrolling posts, alerts)
- [ ] Slide 2: Live product demo or high-fidelity mockups
- [ ] Slide 3: Dashboard screenshot with overlaid tech logos
- [ ] Slide 4: Market TAM breakdown chart
- [ ] Slide 5: Revenue funnel visualization
- [ ] Consistent color scheme (purple/cyan primary, white/dark secondary)
- [ ] Readable font sizes (no text smaller than 18pt)
- [ ] SVCE branding/acknowledgments (if applicable)

### Video Production Workflow
1. **Record Intro** (optional): 5-second hook with you on camera
2. **Record Voiceover**: Each slide separately. One take per slide, multiple takes total.
3. **Edit Together**: Slides timed to voiceover length
4. **Add B-Roll**: Product demo clips, logos, animations
5. **Add Subtitle Captions**: Key points highlighted (for accessibility + engagement)
6. **Export**: 1080p60 or 4K30, optimized for YouTube/Vimeo
